Origami

Origami is the traditional Japanease art of paper folding.

Beginnings

While technically originating in 6th century China, Origami was widely popularized in Japan. It became widespread due to the reduced price of washi paper in the Edo period, from 1603 to 1868.

since then, the art has been practiced throughout the globe, and folders everywhere have pushed it to it's limits, whether it be fully fledged life sized dragons, to people, to geometrically shaped works of art.

Ways Origami is used now.

The ways the art is used today is pretty much endless.

  • Used traditionally in decoration. you've probably seen many cranes hang around like flags.
  • used to display as a form of art, typically in conventions.
  • used to give to someone as a gift, made from either paper, or copied using minerals, or clay.
  • to use as a pastime when you're bored, and don't want to focus and work(me).

there are other ways to use it outside of artistically. Some folder create practical models used to:

  1. play a game, or compete with.
  2. as a fidget toy.
  3. moves in order to "breath life" into the models.
